Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti is a classic Italian pasta dish made with long, thin noodles that are commonly served with a flavorful tomato-based meat sauce. Traditional recipes often include ground beef or Italian sausage, tomatoes, onions, garlic, herbs, and Parmesan cheese, creating a hearty and comforting meal enjoyed around the world.

Known for its simple ingredients and rich flavor, spaghetti is a popular choice for family dinners and special gatherings. It is often served with garlic bread, a fresh salad, or extra grated Parmesan cheese, making it a satisfying and versatile dish that is both easy to prepare and delicious.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Diced tomatoes (optional)
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, then stir in the minced garlic.
  4. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon. Drain any excess grease if needed.
  5. Stir in the tomato sauce, diced tomatoes (if using), Italian seasoning, salt, and black pepper.
  6. Let the sauce simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss until the noodles are evenly coated.
  8. Serve the spaghetti with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped parsley, if desired.
