Chicken Alfredo

A chicken alfredo dish in a bowel

Chicken Alfredo is a classic Italian-American pasta dish made with tender pieces of chicken, fettuccine noodles, and a rich, creamy Alfredo sauce. The sauce is typically prepared with butter, heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and garlic, creating a smooth and flavorful coating for the pasta.

This comforting meal is known for its creamy texture and savory taste, making it a favorite for family dinners and special occasions. It is often served with garlic bread or a fresh salad, making it a simple yet satisfying dish that is both filling and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1–2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 8–12 ounces fettuccine pasta
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the fettuccine according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and black pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken until golden brown and fully cooked. Remove from the skillet and slice into strips.
  4. Melt the butter in the same skillet, then add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream and bring it to a gentle simmer.
  6. Gradually stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
  7. Add the cooked fettuccine to the skillet and toss until the pasta is evenly coated with the Alfredo sauce.
  8. Place the sliced chicken on top of the pasta and gently mix if desired.
  9. Garnish with chopped parsley and extra Parmesan cheese before serving.
